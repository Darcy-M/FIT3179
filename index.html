A Guide to Tourism in Japan

Japan has become one of the most popular tourist destinations of recent times, and the Land of the Rising Sun has certainly found it's way onto the bucket lists of many intrepid travellers due to it's bustling cities, innovative technology, and beautiful landscapes. In this page, I plan to guide you through not only the best places to visit, but also to give some advice about how to get the most out of your unforgettable journey!




Some Context

Where does Japan sit on the world stage?

While the heavy-hitters in world tourism remain European countries like France and Spain, there is no doubt that Japan is quickly catching up.


This first graph compares Japanese international arrivals to other major tourist countries. This number has grown from 7 million in 2006, to an incredible 31 million in 2018. It is now almost as popular a tourist destination as the UK!

This second graph shows the results from a 2017 World Economic Forum study, ranking countries' performance in several key categories out of 7. Japan had the best railroad infrastructure in the world, and ranks comfortably in the top 15 for tourism competitiveness and World Heritage sites.

What areas should I visit?

The best thing about Japan is the variety! You can spend your time entirely in cities, or by soaking in the natural wonders.


If you're more of a city slicker, the darker suburbs (where the population density is high) might be your go-to! Otherwise, places like Hokkaido have a lot of natural beauty.

Skiing is one of Japan's most famed pastimes! Naturally the north is where you want to be for the best skiing, but Nagano also has a large number of ski resorts.

Buddhist temples are a must-see part of Japan. Some of the oldest and most beautiful structures in the world, you might want to go somewhere packed full of them, such as Aichi.
